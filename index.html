Advanced Baseline Imager Live Imaging of Vegetated Ecosystems (ALIVE)

Estimating terrestrial carbon dioxide uptake in real time from a geostationary platform.

The Advanced Baseline Imager Live Imaging of Vegetated Ecosystems (ALIVE) project is lead by Dr. Paul Stoy through the Biological Systems Engineering program at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

Satellite remote sensing is central to our understanding of the terrestrial carbon cycle and its recent changes. We are working to estimate carbon fluxes in real time using geostationary "weather" satellites, especially GOES-R (the Geostationary Operational Environmental Satellite - R Series). All of our code is open access and uses open-access data, with recent outputs and results described here. We would love to collaborate, please be in touch!
